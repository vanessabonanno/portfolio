Building Social Cohesion

Digital Heritage and Multimedia

  • Category: Final dissertation (MA); Research study
  • Keywords: cohesion, participation, inclusion, digital heritage
  • Project date: 14 March, 2023; data pubblicazion
  • Link: SCIRES paper; Online presentation
  • What I did:
  • drafting questionnaires
  • dispensing surveys
  • interviews
  • behavioural observation
  • qualitative/quantitative analysis of data

Project description

Social Cohesion (SC) is today a priority at different levels. Cultural Heritage can be the ideal context to promote it, through interactive digital technologies designed to engage groups of visitors. The purpose of the present study is to identify how to design digital heritage applications for SC and how to measure SC. The results are based on the design of a collaborative and hybrid prototype, Brancacci POV (Point of View), and on the analysis of 107 visitors who have tried the experience in groups of 5 participants guided/moderated by an expert. From this analysis, it emerges that: it is possible to strengthen S.C. with ‚Äúout-groups‚Äù when social/collaborative tasks are included in the experience; that it is possible to shorten the distance between citizens and (cultural) institutions adopting ‚Äúmediated dialogue‚Äù approaches; that it focus, motivation, trust and ‚Äúin-group‚Äù perception of inclusion emerge when digital heritage experiences are set in intimate and quiet environments.

The study addresses the relationship between Cultural Heritage and Social Cohesion, and it aims at defining innovative design strategies to strengthen Social Cohesion and at measuring ‚Äúcohesiveness‚Äù within digital collaborative experiences in the Cultural Heritage context. Social Cohesion is defined as ‚Äúan ongoing process of developing well-being, sense of belonging, and voluntary social participation of the members of society, while developing communities that tolerate and promote a multiplicity of values and cultures and granting at the same time equal rights and opportunities in society‚Äù (Fonseca et al., 2019, 246). The measures used in the experiments are mainly based on the frameworks described by Dragolov et al. (2016) and Fonseca et al. (2019). The study is divided into three phases: (1) the definition of measures for Social Cohesion in the Cultural Heritage, through the creation of a ‚Äúcultural probe kit‚Äù; (2) the design of an evaluation tool to test the perception of Social Cohesion on the Brancacci POV interactive experience, with a focus on ingroup contact, participation, and reduction of outgroup distance; (3) the definition of design strategies to adopt in future interactive digital heritage tools for fostering Social Cohesion. The main findings have demonstrated that the perception of cohesiveness concerning participation and connectedness with others is high. Most of the visitors reported feeling a ‚Äúdistance‚Äù from specific outgroups (such as Cultural Heritage professionals, museum curators and restorers) before, and declared to feel closer to them after the Brancacci POV experience. Additionally, visitors participating with strangers declared experiencing a sense of connection with other members and feeling part of the group. These promising results suggest developing specific design strategies, such as the integration of highly collaborative tasks and different roles to assign to every group member to increase participation and sense of inclusion; the introduction of the moderator figure to help and support; the choice of intimate and quiet environments in which to try the experience, to improve focus and motivation, and to boost a sense of empathy, trust, and inclusion within the group.

Project description

This is the final project "Different twins" for the course User Experience of DHDK, University of Bologna. The main aim of the project is to use the interface to trasmit two separate (and complementary) messages at the same time. ¬´Different twins is the design of a web site / web application / mobile app / desktop application meant to allow an organization, company, or community to deliver two different, complementary messages from the same interface.¬ª

Our solution was to integrate a service of hyper-personalisation that will support the consumers' buying experience, guiding the users in the selection of the best formula and products based on their needs. Hyperpersonalisation is an extension of standard personalisation. Whereas personalisation entails a thorough knowledge and use of customer personas, data, and analytics, hyper-personalisation makes use of real-time data and artificial intelligence to create even more unique buyer experiences. These personalised experiences may be used to bring up relevant contents, products, or services that depend on the current buyer behaviour.

Specifically, I worked on the user research (online surveys, interviews), user testing (testing protocol, analysis of results, urgency curve), personas (protagonist, secondary, additional) and scenarios, design proposal (info architecture, user testing on the new design, recommendations, final report).

Project description

The dissertation ‚ÄúIl progressive rock in Italia: storia, analisi e organologia elettronica di Per un amico‚Äù aims to analyze the different aspects of the album Per un Amico by Premiata Forneria Marconi. The thesis begins with the history of progressive rock genre, from its birth to its spread in Italy, with a focus on its characteristics. In this context, Premiata Forneria Marconi became quickly one of the most noted popular bands in the Country: with the release of Per un Amico the band increased its notoriety abroad and was able to leave for England. After the introduction of the historical events that lead to the conception of Per un Amico, there is the analysis of the five tracks of the album: every song is examinated under its musical and lyrical aspects. The next chapter is dedicated to the Mellotron and the Minimoog; it anticipates the multimedia project.

The project consists in three codes written on CSound that demonstrate how to synthesize and how to sample three different Moog solos, taken from Per un Amico, Appena un Po' and Il Banchetto. The last part of this chapter presents the main differences between the original solo and its digital reproduction. The very last chapter is about the influences of Premiata Forneria Marconi nowadays. The interview with Lars Fredrik Fr√∏islie highlights how the italian band has became a model for some progressive rock bands of our time.

A role-playing game social network analysis

Network analysis

  • Category: Final project for "Web science" (MA)
  • Keywords: network analysis, Gephi
  • Project date: 2022
  • Link: Github repository
  • What I did:
  • characters' research
  • scenarios
  • gephi networks and analysis
  • final report

Project description

The paper unfolds the "Fallout: New Vegas" videogame events with a social network analysis approach thanks to five different scenarios, revealing nodes relationships and their differences. In particular, the nodes correspond to the game characters and the edges represent their bonds. Network and nodes measures such as centrality, nodes positions, density, modularity, and components are applied to study every scenario situation. Finally, there is an analysis about which of the four endings might be the most balanced one and why.

This paper was also possible thanks to Tommaso Scarton, who dedicated lots of his days to searching to the characters' background and useful information.

Project description

FOOData was created for the Open Access and Digital Ethics exam by Vanessa Bonanno, Giorgia Samp√≤, and Sarah Tew. FOOData explores food production, pollution, and health markers within Europe both individually and together to get a clearer picture of their interlation. FOOData uses geospatial visualizations to reconnect users with the geographic reality of food production and pollution.

This project aims to show connections between food production, environment and health conditions in European countries from 2009 to 2019, focusing in particular on different countries' production of food, their health, and pollution status. We hypothesize that areas with more agricultural environmental damage will have worse health outcomes and that different types of pollution and food production affect health differently. To do so we collected data from EUROSTAT, the European Environmental Agency and FAO.
